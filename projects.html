Online Database Index Selection Using Metrical Task Systems

Date: July 2024 - October 2024 | Institution: University of Melbourne

This research project formed my Master's thesis at the University of Melbourne, supervised by Dr. William Umboh.

I took the Advanced Algorithms and Data Structures subject (COMP90077) during Semester 1 of 2024, where I was introduced to some very interesting online algorithms, including one for the set cover problem (utilizing linear programming). William was one of the subject coordinators, and he introduced me to the idea of Metrical Task Systems (MTS) and had been contemplating using it to develop an algorithm for database index selection. This ultimately culminated in my thesis, where I expanded on William's idea and developed a unique MTS-based approach for database index selection. I then demonstrated empirically that my prototype performed exceptionally well when benchmarked against a current state-of-the-art Multi-Armed Bandit-based algorithm.

The project combined elements from a diverse set of topics including: combinatorial optimization, online algorithms, machine learning and AI, recommender systems, and database tuning. It was an incredibly rewarding experience that not only brought together the knowledge and skills I had acquired from my coursework but also challenged me to push the boundaries of my abilities and explore new ideas.
